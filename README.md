## Assignment Details

### Group Project - Part 1
Problem Statements- one submission per group!
Describe at least three problems related to health and wellness that your team is interested in designing solutions for. Include an assessment of the pros and cons for each of the potential problems. Note that we are not yet talking about solutions (what you will decide), but rather what needs your design will address. 

Prepare a document addressing the following points for each problem:

What makes the project interesting? What is the design problem or research question related to a particular health/wellness context?

What is the pain point you want to solve, the new capability you want to enable, or the research you want to explore?

What existing understanding of the problem has been developed?



### Group Project - Part 2
Part I

Describe the final problem that your team is interested in creating designs for your final project.  Create a work plan for the project and include a plan for who will be taking what role for each of the deliverables to make sure that the work is being distributed among the team. 

Part II: Target Population Research and Competitive Analysis

Include the following:

Background research on your target population
Empathizing with and understanding of needs from your target population
Competitive Analysis of your problem
Your background research on your target population should include:

A paragraph description of who your target population is:
Who is a part of your population?
What are things you are considering putting in your prototype that will cater towards your population?
Since most of your populations are a sensitive subset, what are you keeping in mind to address this?
Any other necessary information about your population we should know. 
Empathizing with and understanding of needs from your target population:

Identify online resources (blogs, vlogs, online communities) for your target population that you can use to gain empathy for and identify the needs of your target population.  
Include the links, pdfs, etc. of the resources. 
Write 1-2 sentence descriptions of each resource. 

Create a list of themes or codes from your analysis of these resources and describe how these themes inform your understanding of people's needs. 
Your competitive analysis of your problem should include:

Since you have now defined the problem(s) you want to solve, the next step is to examine the existing solutions to those problems so that you know what you are competing against, and where the gaps/opportunities are. It's OK if there are existing solutions to your problem--it's rare to find a problem that no one has ever tried to solve before. Your job in this section is to analyze any existing solutions there are and explain why they fall short.

Re-state your problem statement, after this, include another section that lists at least 3 of the most popular existing solutions to your problem. Briefly describe each solution, and then analyze it by answering the following:

Why do people use this, and what does it do well?
How does it fall short?
How could your solution do better?
This section should also be concise, about 1-2 pages covering at least 3 competitors. Someone who would be considering whether to fund your project would look at this to ensure that you've studied the space and know where the opportunities are.



### Group Project - Part 3

Part I:

Submit detailed (hand-drawn) sketches of your prototype -- at least 10 sketches of the user interfaces you will be implementing in the next stage (each group member should contribute sketches).
Sketches of your prototype:
This sketch should include the layout of the main parts of your prototype. 
Your sketches do not need to be polished, but they should clearly and concisely convey your plans and provide a sense for where you are headed with your prototype.
Descriptions of your prototype presented with necessary screenshots of the sketches.
Part II:

A description of the key features supported by your design
Are you planning on making a mobile, web, physical prototype?
Who is responsible for what parts of your prototype?
In addition, feel free to describe any challenges that you are facing in your project.



### Group Project - Part 4
For this assignment you should submit:

A description of how your current more developed prototype has changed/developed since Part 3 submission. Please include side by side screenshots of previous sketch to new prototype (7.5 pts)

Discuss in a few sentences what design challenges you faced if any, what informed your design and how you came up with the close to final prototype (7.5 pts)
